“100 hours of ML” is a community of DigitalGlobe employees committed to completing 100 hours of self-paced learning on Machine Learning.

To get started:

  1. Join our us on slack #100-hours-of-ml
  2. Browse the best resources (below) for learning ML curated by DigitalGlobe team members

Community

#100-hours-of-ml slack channel serves as a community for people to ask questions, share valuable resources, and share their progress on this ML learning journey. Join our us on slack #100-hours-of-ml

Resources

Start here
  • Google ML Crash Course: Overall introduction
  • Berkeley ML Crash Course: Terminology
  • FastAI

    “Fast ai gets your hands dirty quickly and is more focused on deep learning.” -- @johnny.riskas

    "a practical approach... writing code sooner than later — fastai courses are probably a good example of this, but unfortunately make heavy use of their own libraries" -- @colllin

Complete Courses:
  • Foundations of Data Science
  • Principles and Techniques of Data Science
  • Intro to AI

    “For first timers, it was accessible and did a good job of explaining fundamentals and helping you to visualize their effects.” -- @kaya.woodall

  • Udemy Machine Learning A to Z

    “I found the Udemy AZ courses useful for learning more about the Python Libraries associated with Machine Learning (SK Learn, Pytorch, TensorFlow, Keras). I personally found the math/theory in these classes to be a little light, but I think for most people interested in learning the fundamentals of ML, it will more than suffice. There are a couple of good image classifier examples in each course that I found particularly useful for my work at DG.” -- @chloe

  • Stanford ML Course (free if not taken for credit)

    “Stanford was good for people who literally know nothing about ML because you need some foundations.” -- @johnny.riskas

    “Stanford ML course was really helpful. It really provided good foundation, at least for me.” -- @spervez

  • Amazon Machine Learning University
  • Coursera Andrew Ng’s ML course
  • Coursera Deep Learning Specialization

    "I thought Coursera’s DL course was poorly done" -- @johhny.riskas

  • Udacity DL Nano Degree Program

    “Udacity's DL nano had good practical projects and did a review of some of the most popular architectures” -- @johhny.riskas

  • Bloomberg ML Training

    “The Bloomberg "Foundations of Machine Learning" training course is designed for internal company's software engineers. There are 30 lectures which are comprehensive and move at a good pace. The key is that these are challenging and the lecture time is well spent covering each concept. There are supplied Lecture Slides. Understanding these concepts gives a good foundation when applied.” -- @mikesmith
